Project Goals / Problem Statement

The goal of Unmask was to design a dating app experience that encourages authentic connections, prioritizes user privacy, and simplifies the onboarding process while differentiating it from it's competitors. The project challenged me to think critically about UX flows, accessibility, and interactive design.

User Personas

To better understand the target audience, I created two personas, Alex and Jordan, representing neurodivergent adults navigating dating. They highlighted key needs, goals, and frustrations, like sensory sensitivities and a desire for meaningful connections, and guided design decisions to make Unmask inclusive and user-centered.

User Journey Maps

User journey maps highlighted key pain points and motivations, revealing opportunities to create smoother, sensory-friendly interactions. These insights guided interface decisions and feature design to support meaningful connections.

Research Insights & Direction

The research phase established a clear foundation for the Unmask experience. Through a competitive audit of existing dating platforms and analysis of user feedback, key gaps emerged around accessibility, emotional safety, and authentic connection, particularly for neurodivergent users.

User personas and journey maps helped translate these insights into concrete design priorities, revealing moments of cognitive overload, social pressure, and friction within typical dating flows. These findings directly informed the decision to design Unmask as a sensory-friendly dating app focused on clarity, intentional pacing, and meaningful connections rather than surface-level interactions.

By grounding design decisions in research, Unmask evolved from a general dating concept into a purpose-driven product tailored to the needs of neurodivergent users.

Logo and Brand Design

For the Unmask brand, I began by prompting AI tools to explore initial logo concepts centered around the idea of masking and identity. I then iterated the design through three refinements, improving clarity, accessibility, and visual balance at each stage.

The final logo was designed to align with the app's values, while the color palette was carefully selected to minimize sensory overload and create a welcoming, friendly experience for neurodivergent users.

Paper Wireframes

The paper wireframes served as a critical foundation for Unmask's design decisions. I began by sketching five variations of each core page, allowing me to quickly explore different layouts, information hierarchies, and interaction flows without the constraints of digital tools.

From these initial concepts, I created refined wireframes as needed, narrowing in on solutions that supported clarity, reduced cognitive load, and aligned with the app's accessibility goals before moving into high-fidelity design.

Low-Fidelity Figma Prototype

After creating an initial low-fidelity prototype in Figma, I gathered peer feedback to identify usability issues and opportunities for improvement. This feedback helped refine layout clarity, reduce cognitive load, and improve the overall flow of key interactions.

Before Feedback

  • Navigation felt unclear
  • User profile needed detailed preferences
  • Design lacked visual hierarchy

After Feedback

  • Added Nav bar for clearer navigation
  • User profile details added
  • Improved visual design
